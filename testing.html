International Perspective

As per FAO, India is the second largest producer of paddy globally after China followed by Indonesia. In 2021, China produced 214.43 MMT of paddy, India produced 169.14 MMT followed by Indonesia at 55.25 MMT.

According to the World Bank Pink Sheet data, global rice prices (Thai 25 % broken) reached an average of USD 585/MT in March 2024. Prices are lower month on month by 5% compared to Februrary 2024 when prices were USD 614/MT and are higher 32% year on year.

The USDA's latest projections for the 2023/24 period indicate global rice production at 515.73 MMT, slightly higher than the earlier estimate of 515.39 MMT. As of April 1, 2024, global ending stocks were 172.15 MMT.

INFLATION:

WPI inflation: Inflation in the Wholesale Price Index in Feb 2024 was 10.28% higher than 11.53% reported in the same month last year and higher from 7.51% reported in previous month.

CPI inflation: Usually, CPI inflation in rice tends to be at its lowest level in in Oct, Nov or Dec, which coincides with the kharif crop’s harvest. It rises thereafter, and arrival of rabi & summer crop tends to moderate the rising trend around July or Aug & CPI inflation peaks in Sept, right before the onset of the next kharif crop. Inflation in the Consumer Price Index in Feb 2024 was 12.90% which is higher than 11.22% reported in the same month last year and also higher than 12.94% reported in previous month.
